📜 Top 12 Password Security Rules

1
Different Password for Every Site: Never reuse passwords across different accounts.
2
Minimum 12 Characters: Longer passwords are exponentially harder to crack.
3
Mix Character Types: Use uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and symbols.
4
Enable 2FA: Two-Factor Authentication is your second line of defense.
5
Use a Password Manager: Don't try to remember them all; use a trusted vault.
6
Avoid Personal Info: Don't use birthdays, pet names, or phone numbers.
7
Update Important Passwords: Change your bank and email passwords every 6 months.
8
Check for Breaches: Use HaveIBeenPwned to check if your data was leaked.
9
Don't Share Passwords: Never give your password to anyone, even "support".
10
Beware of Phishing: Always check the URL before entering your password.
11
Secure Your Recovery Email: Your recovery email is the keys to your kingdom.
12
Logout on Public Devices: Always sign out and clear history on shared computers.

🛡️ Common Password Attacks — Explained

Brute Force: Attacker tries every possible combination of characters until they find the right one.
Dictionary Attack: Using a list of common words and previously leaked passwords to guess yours.
Phishing: Fake websites that look like the real ones to trick you into entering your credentials.
Keylogging: Malware that records every keystroke you type on your keyboard.
Credential Stuffing: Using passwords leaked from one site to try and login to your other accounts.
Rainbow Table Attack: Pre-computed hash tables used to reverse password hashes quickly.
Social Engineering: Manipulating people into revealing passwords through deception and trust exploitation.
Man-in-the-Middle: Intercepting communication between you and the website to steal credentials in transit.

✅ Quick Start Security Checklist

✓
Install a password manager — Bitwarden or KeePassXC recommended
✓
Enable 2FA on all important accounts — Email, bank, social media
✓
Check HaveIBeenPwned — See if your email was in any data breach
✓
Install uBlock Origin — Block ads, trackers, malware domains
✓
Use encrypted DNS — Cloudflare 1.1.1.1 or Quad9
✓
Update all software regularly — OS, browser, apps — enable auto-update
✓
Backup important data — Use 3-2-1 rule: 3 copies, 2 media, 1 offsite
✓
Review app permissions on phone — Remove unnecessary camera/mic/location access
✓
Delete unused accounts — Use JustDeleteMe to find deletion links
✓
Secure your home WiFi — WPA3, strong password, disable WPS
